QTPy

by John Driscoll

QTPy is an implementation of
Quantum tic-tac-toe in Python and HTML5.

You can install this game on your iPhone/iPod by tapping the "+" sign, then "Add to Home Screen".

How to play

  • A turn consists of placing two "spooky marks" on the board. You can put spooky marks in any empty square, and any square containing other spooky marks. The two squares marked in each turn are then "linked".

  • If a player creates a cycle of linked squares during his turn, the next player must make a measurement of a square in that cycle. The square measured will "collapse" to contain the mark of the player making the measurement:

  • If the cycle is all the same color, the cycle is automatically collapsed:

  • Any marks occuring inside a cycle will be collapsed, even if they aren't links in the cycle:

  • The game ends after any collapsed marks cause a classical tic-tac-toe on the board:

    If two tic-tac-toes are formed in the same move, the player whose row contains the faster of the slowest spooky marks gets 1 point, and the other gets ½ of a point.

You can find a thorough explanation of the QTTT rules on the Wikipedia page.
